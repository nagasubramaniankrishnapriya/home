So first, what are oceans?

Oceans were created when gases started to appear on our planet. In the starting, there was no oxygen or atmosphere. After it formed, it went through a tumultuous period of being continually hit by rocks and meteoroids. Our Earth was able to create it's crust, but with that came the issue of colliding tectonic plates, and volcanic eruptions, leading to the atmosphere forming. And finally, it received rain, and water which slowly filled up the world's pits, and created oceans, lakes, and the such.

For this reason we are one of a kind. Many planets have large bodies of water, but Earth is unique for the fact that it has water, liquid water, on the surface.
